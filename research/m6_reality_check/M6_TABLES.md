## T8 — Gate ablation (calibration injections recall / null FP)
| variant | recall | null FP rate |
|---|---|---|
| **sealed** (FAP gate + sign + shape vetting) | 0.133 | **0.000** |
| no_shape (drop sign + shape vetting) | 0.189 | 0.000 |
| no_fap_gate (drop bootstrap period-FAP gate) | 0.167 | **0.123** |

**Reading:** the **bootstrap period-FAP gate is what controls false positives** — removing it lets the null FP rate jump 0.0 → 12.3 %. The **shape/sign vetting is conservative** — it trims recall by ~5.5 pp (0.189 → 0.133) on clean injections; its false-positive benefit is on *astrophysical* contaminants (it rejected 4/8 EBs in T6), not on clean nulls. Together they defend novel-contribution #4 (calibrated photometric gating) and rebut the v3 "FP = noise artifact" critique (R3). *(Recall here is cheap-confirm-only across variants — a relative comparison, not the combined-arm recall of T2.)*

The cheap confirmer **systematically underestimates depth (~20 %)** and the detector's seed duration is **~31 % short** (coarse duration grid; Figure F5_depth). These biases are part of why the cheap path's parameters are less precise than a full TLS fit — and connect to the right-period/wrong-epoch loss pathway (§3.2).
